My research

I'm doing a PhD with the Climate Change Research Centre (CCRC) at the University of New South Wales, Sydney. I investigate the roles temperature and humidity play on human health.

Recent work in this area has seen researchers develop heat stress indices, such as the Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT), and heatwave indices, such as Excess Heat Factor (EHF). I'm interested in combining these approaches to find the best way to predict the impacts of heat stress.

My work primarily revolves around working with climatological data sets (series of observed and modelled variables) and epidemiological data sets (time series of health indicators, like hospital admissions). I keep sharp skills in languages like R and Bash. I'm hoping to pick up Python soon!

I spend most of my time with the Geophysical Fluid Dynamics lab at the Australian National University. If you'd like to chat about research, climate change or other academic matters, feel free to hit me up at j.goldie@unsw.edu.au.
